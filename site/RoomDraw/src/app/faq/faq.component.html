What is Room Draw? **NOTE ALL DATA IS FROM LAST YEAR'S ROOM DRAW INFO**

Room Draw is Benedictine College's annual housing selection process. All students are required to participate in Room Draw in order to select their campus housing for the following year. Room Draw is based on seniority, determined by social class standing (senior, junior, and sophomore). Starting with the senior class, each member of a class is assigned a random number which determines his or her bidding order (see bidding order below). Those students with the lowest numbers bid first. An individual's random number can be seen on the left side of the home page of the Room Draw website after logging in.

What do I have to do to participate in Room Draw?

  1. Find your name and random number on the home page of the Room Draw website.

What is the bidding order for Room Draw?

Bidding order is determined by the social class standing of the members of a housing group, which students can form. The exact order is listed below:

  1. Senior and Senior
  2. Senior and Junior
  3. Senior, Junior, and Sophomore
  4. Senior and Sophomore
  5. Junior and Junior
  6. Junior and Sophomore
  7. Sophomore and Sophomore

Who participates in Room Draw?

All traditional, undergraduate students, regardless of their previous housing arrangements are required to apply for housing each year, including those with exceptions for living off-campus. Students should not sign any lease agreements for off-campus housing before receiving written approval to live off-campus from the Student Life Office. Students who enter into lease agreements prior to Room Draw or without written approval from the Student Life Office do so at their own risk.

What if I am studying abroad the semester Room Draw occurs?

Students currently studying abroad follow the same procedure as non-study abroad students to apply for housing.

Are there any factors that would affect my random number, my right to participate in Room Draw, or my right to select certain housing?

Students on disciplinary probation or who violate Room Draw procedures in the current academic year will be assigned a number that falls at the end of their class. They are also not eligible to live in the Row Houses. Students who fail to participate in Room Draw forefeit the right to choose a room or roommate and will be assigned a room by the college.

What if I am graduating in the fall of 2020 and/or am a fifth year senior?

Fall 2020 graduates are required to live off-campus. Fall graduates who would still like to live on-campus for their final semester can be added as an additional roommate to a full living option (i.e. they can be a fifth person in a four-person apartment).

5th year seniors can live on campus if they commit to a full year. 5th year seniors can be a member of any group and do not receive a random number for Room Draw.
